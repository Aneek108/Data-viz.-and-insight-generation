1. Edible mushrooms often have smooth or silky stalk surfaces above and below the ring. In contrast, poisonous mushrooms might have scaly or fibrous stalk surfaces.

2. Mushrooms with foul, fishy, or pungent odors are often poisonous. In contrast, edible mushrooms typically have mild or pleasant odors like almond or anise, or no odor at all.

3. Mushrooms with white, brown, or black spore prints are typically safer to eat compared to those with unusual spore print colors like green or purple, which are often associated with toxic species.

4. Brightly colored caps, such as red, yellow, violet or green, often indicate poisonous mushrooms. Edible mushrooms usually have more subdued cap colors like brown, white, or gray.
